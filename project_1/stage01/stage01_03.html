The New Typography [1923]
                 — Laszlo Moholy-Nagy


T              Typography is a tool of communication.
h               It must be communication in its most
e               intense form. The emphasis must be on
                 absolute clarity since this distinguishes
N              the character of our own writing from
e               that of ancient pictographic forms.
w       
                 Our intellectual relationship to the world
T               is individual-exact (e.g.,this individual-exact
y                relationship is in a state of transition
p               toward a collective-exact orientation).
o               This is in contrast to the ancient individual-
g                amorphous and later collective-amorphous
r                mode of communication.
a       
p               Therefore priority: unequivocal clarity in
h               all typographical compositions. Legibility-
y               communication must never be impaired by
                 an a priori aesthetics.
